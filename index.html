About

A creative director
rooted in craft.

I'm Shaakira Titus, an Art Director with a soft spot for pixels, paper, and everything in between.

My creative adventure began in the world of print — back when deadlines were measured in ink and print runs. But curiosity got the best of me, and I soon dived headfirst into the wild, ever-evolving realm of digital design. Along the way, I picked up all sorts of creative tricks — from crafting animations and countless web banners, to coding mountains of mailers in basic HTML, designing websites, apps (both UI & UX), as well as digital magazines. I also refined my video editing skills through Adobe Premiere Pro training, with a focus on editing principles, workflow and time-based effects.

That curiosity turned into leadership when I started guiding my team and company through their own digital transformations — developing digital design guidelines, running training sessions, and helping everyone embrace the new creative frontier. Turns out, I didn't just grow as a designer — I grew into a digital storyteller with a pretty solid bag of tricks.

I'm currently at Media24 in Cape Town, looking after a high-profile client and making sure creativity meets clarity in every project. Before that, I was part of New Media (a sister company to the Media24 family), where I started out as a digital designer and got to collaborate with amazing brands like Eat Out, Woolworths Taste, Multichoice, Ackermans, Vodacom, Mediclinic, Sanlam, and Visi — and even New Media itself.

Somewhere along the way, I found a new passion — visual internal communications. Shaping how a company talks to its people, builds culture, and expresses its personality became one of the most rewarding parts of my journey. And honestly, I still get excited about that every day.

Latest Award

Recognised for work
that connects.

Internal communications is the heartbeat of any workplace — it's where company culture truly comes to life.

The client needed stronger employee engagement, and I've always believed nothing brings people together quite like food. So, I built the concept around that universal truth. We asked employees to share their most treasured family recipes — the ones that never fail and have been passed down for generations — along with a short story about why the recipe meant so much to them, plus a photo of the person who handed it down.

And wow, did the submissions pour in!

We re-created and photographed every recipe in studio, then had employees vote for their favourite. But I didn't want it to end there. A great campaign should have legs — so we compiled every heartfelt recipe and story into a beautifully designed company recipe book, a tangible keepsake that celebrated the people and culture behind the brand. It was gifted to every employee at year-end.

This campaign didn't just engage — it connected. It tugged at heartstrings, sparked conversations, and gave everyone something truly meaningful to hold onto. We submitted it to the 2024 Eddie & Ozzie Awards — and proudly brought home an Eddie for Custom Content Marketing in New York.
